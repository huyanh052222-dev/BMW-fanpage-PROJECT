History of the Legend Brand

BMW (Bayerische Motoren Werke) was founded in 1916 in Munich, Germany, originally producing aircraft engines. After World War I, the company shifted to manufacturing motorcycles and automobiles.

In 1928, BMW entered the automobile market by acquiring Fahrzeugfabrik Eisenach, which produced the Dixi, a licensed version of the Austin 7. The first car sold under the BMW brand was the BMW 3/15. During World War II, BMW focused on producing aircraft engines for the German military. Post-war, the company faced significant challenges but eventually rebounded, introducing iconic models like the BMW 328 Roadster and the 3 Series. Today, BMW is renowned for its luxury vehicles and performance-oriented cars, with a global presence and a commitment to innovation.
